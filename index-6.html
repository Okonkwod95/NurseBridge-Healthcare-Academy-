About the Programme

About NurseBridge Academy

NurseBridge Academy is committed to providing practical caregiver education focused on developing professional caregiving skills for home and community support environments.

Mission: To develop competent, compassionate, and professional caregivers through quality training.

Why Become a Certified Caregiver?

The demand for caregiving skills continues to grow, both professionally and within families and communities. Professional caregiver education provides the knowledge, confidence, and practical skills needed to support others safely and effectively.

Caring for your loved ones still requires knowledge

Caregiving isn't only for employment. Many people care for parents, grandparents, spouses, children, or relatives without any formal preparation. Professional caregiver education helps individuals provide safer, more effective, and more compassionate support to their loved ones.

Benefits

What you'll gain

  • Gain practical, hands-on caregiving skills
  • Improve your professional opportunities
  • Build confidence in supporting others
  • Learn safe and effective caregiving practices
  • Receive a Professional Caregiver Certificate and Official Academic Transcript
  • Develop valuable life skills that benefit both individuals and families
Suitable for

Individuals caring for elderly parents or relatives, family members supporting loved ones with disabilities, community volunteers, individuals preparing for future caregiving responsibilities, people interested in practical life skills, and individuals seeking professional caregiving opportunities.

Curriculum

Global standard curriculum, module by module

Fully optimized to align with international care frameworks and the UK Care Certificate. Tap a module to see what's covered.

Module 1: Introduction to Caregiving & Your Professional Role 10 Hours
  • Understanding the caregiver's role, professional boundaries, and accountability
  • Working as part of an interdisciplinary healthcare and home care team
  • The importance of worker regulation, codes of conduct, and professional development
Module 2: Advanced Communication & Documentation Skills 15 Hours
  • Overcoming verbal and non-verbal communication barriers
  • Active listening techniques and handling sensitive, confidential information
  • Accurate clinical record-keeping, incident reporting, and multilingual communication adaptations
Module 3: Ethics, Duty of Care, and Professional Conduct 10 Hours
  • Core principles of the Duty of Care and promoting client independence
  • Managing ethical dilemmas, conflicts of interest, and patient rights
  • Understanding confidentiality, data protection, and the right to make choices
Module 4: Personal Care Support, Privacy, and Dignity 25 Hours
  • Assisting with activities of daily living (bathing, grooming, dressing, oral care)
  • Maintaining skin integrity, pressure ulcer prevention, and continence care
  • Upholding privacy, dignity, and respect while delivering intimate personal care
Module 5: Moving and Handling / Mobility Assistance 20 Hours
  • Ergonomic principles of safe lifting, body mechanics, and back care
  • Safe transfer techniques (bed to chair, chair to commode, repositioning)
  • Correct usage of assistive mobility devices, hoists, slide sheets, and walking frames
Module 6: Nutrition, Hydration, and Food Safety 15 Hours
  • Principles of balanced nutrition, dietary requirements, and specialized diets
  • Monitoring fluid intake, recognizing signs of dehydration and malnutrition
  • Hygienic food preparation, handling, and assisting individuals with swallowing difficulties (dysphagia)
Module 7: Home Safety, Wellbeing, and Infection Prevention & Control 15 Hours
  • Identifying household hazards, risk assessment, and maintaining safe environments
  • Infection prevention, universal precautions, hand hygiene, and cross-contamination prevention
  • Correct use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safe management of clinical waste
Module 8: Elderly Care & Adult Safeguarding 25 Hours
  • Physical, psychological, and social aspects of aging
  • Managing age-related chronic conditions (arthritis, hypertension, diabetes)
  • Recognizing, preventing, and reporting signs of elder abuse, exploitation, or neglect
Module 9: Disability Support & Person-Centered Care 15 Hours
  • Caring for individuals with physical, sensory, neurodivergent, or learning disabilities
  • Promoting social inclusion, autonomy, and implementing individual support plans
  • The principles of person-centered care, ensuring the individual remains at the center of all choices
Module 10: Child, Family, and Safeguarding Support 10 Hours
  • Basic child development milestones and pediatric care dynamics
  • Family-centered care models, communication with parents, and emotional support
  • Recognizing indicators of child abuse, domestic vulnerability, and reporting procedures
Module 11: Mental Health, Dementia, and Emotional Wellbeing 10 Hours
  • Introduction to common mental health conditions (depression, anxiety, psychosis)
  • Core principles of person-centered dementia care and memory support
  • De-escalation strategies and managing behaviors that challenge, with empathy and patience
Module 12: Basic Life Support, Practical Simulations & Competency Assessment 10 Hours
  • Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) for adults, children, and infants
  • Choking management, basic first aid, and managing medical emergencies (seizures, fainting)
  • Simulated patient scenarios and final direct observation of practical clinical competencies
Assessment

Grading framework

  • Continuous Assessment (30%) — Quizzes, reflective logbooks, case studies, and classroom participation
  • Practical Competency Assessment (40%) — Direct observation of physical skills (hand hygiene technique, safe moving and handling, CPR simulation) during clinical placement
  • Final Examination (30%) — Comprehensive written evaluation covering all theoretical modules
